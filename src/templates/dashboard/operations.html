Post Overdose Response Team (PORT)

Emergency medical resources are most effective when aligned with when and where crises occur. At the Port Angeles Fire Department, operational metrics illuminate a clear truth: overdoses do not follow business hours. To optimize Community Paramedic (CPM) coverage and improve outcomes, we used time-based overdose data to reevaluate our existing staffing model.

Our analysis reveals critical gaps in current Community Paramedic coverage. Traditional 8AM-4PM schedules miss the majority of overdose incidents, which cluster during early evening and morning hours when people are most vulnerable.

  • Data-Driven Insights. Analysis of 122 tracked overdose cases reveals optimal scheduling patterns and coverage gaps in our current system.
  • Strategic Scheduling. 4×10 shift models double our coverage while providing staff with improved work-life balance and 3-day weekends.
  • Measurable Impact. Implementation could increase coverage from 43 to 88+ cases annually, representing a 105% improvement in community reach.

The following analysis demonstrates how strategic scheduling alignment with overdose patterns can maximize Community Paramedic effectiveness while maintaining operational efficiency and staff satisfaction.

Evidence-Based Operational Strategy

By leveraging comprehensive overdose timing data, we've identified optimal deployment windows that align Community Paramedic availability with peak crisis periods. This approach maximizes our intervention opportunities while supporting sustainable staffing models.

Key Insights from Analytics

Peak Risk Hours

Overdoses peak during evening hours (16:00-20:00) and early morning (02:00-06:00), outside current coverage windows.

Weekend Patterns

Weekend overdoses show different timing patterns, with higher evening peaks requiring adjusted coverage strategies.

Optimal Strategy

Staggered 4×10 Monday-Friday shifts (08:00-18:00) provide the highest coverage efficiency while maintaining staff work-life balance, potentially reaching 88 of 122 tracked cases.

Implementation Strategy

Phase 1: Implement 4×10 Staggered C model (Mon–Thu/Tue–Fri 08:00–18:00) - balanced transition with 70% coverage
Phase 2: Monitor coverage metrics, staff satisfaction, and operational efficiency for 6 months
Phase 3: Upgrade to 4×10 Staggered A model (07:00–17:00) for 78% coverage and Friday evening inclusion
Phase 4: Evaluate weekend overlap scenarios if resources permit and data supports expansion
