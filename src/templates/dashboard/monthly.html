Why Track Fatal and Non-Fatal Overdoses Over Time?

Identifying and understanding patterns in overdose types allows Community Paramedics to optimize outreach and intervention strategies.

  • Fatal Overdose Reduction. Clallam County's fatal overdose rate dropped from 73.3 to 34.8 per 100,000 from March 2024 to March 2025—a 63% decrease.
  • Early Intervention Success. PORT's early intervention model yielded a 78% linkage to SUD services when CPMs responded on scene.
  • Repeat Overdose Prevention. Repeat overdoses were drastically reduced when overdose survivors were met by CPMs trained to handle precipitated withdrawal.
  • Real-Time Public Health Response. CPM data informs real-time public health responses and strengthens grant applications.

Time-Based Analysis Benefits

Monthly trend analysis reveals seasonal patterns, helps predict resource needs, and enables proactive intervention strategies. Understanding when overdoses occur most frequently allows for optimal staff scheduling and community outreach timing.

Program Impact Measurement

By tracking overdose trends over time, we can measure the effectiveness of our interventions, identify successful strategies, and adjust our approach based on real-world outcomes. This data-driven approach ensures continuous improvement in our community health programs.
