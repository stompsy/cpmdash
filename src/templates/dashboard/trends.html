  • Problem. Port Angeles faces a public health crisis from rising opioid overdoses:
    • Clallam County reached 73.3 overdose deaths per 100,000 in early 2024.
    • Survivors often refused treatment due to withdrawal after high-dose naloxone.
    • Repeat overdoses and EMS fatigue strained emergency response systems.
  • Solution. Operation Shielding Hope connects overdose survivors to care in real time:
    • Community Paramedics administer buprenorphine in the field.
    • 78% of survivors accepted referral when CPMs were present (vs. 3% without).
    • Funded by the University of Washington and Opioid Settlement grants.
  • Rationale. Ongoing overdose tracking informs better decisions:
    • Reveals high-risk areas and changing overdose patterns.
    • Guides resource allocation and targeted outreach.
    • Strengthens grant applications and program accountability.

Through trusted partnerships with PAPD, ReDiscovery, Reflections Counseling, and others, CPMs coordinate services that save lives and relieve pressure on emergency units. Their work exemplifies how public safety-led outreach can turn a moment of crisis into a turning point toward recovery.

Redefining the Response

Operation Shielding Hope represents a shift in how our city responds to addiction. It’s a model rooted in empathy, connection, and evidence-based care—and it’s working. With your continued support, Port Angeles is not only confronting the opioid crisis, but leading the way with a public health-driven solution.

Opioid Settlement Funds

In May of 2022, Attorney General Bob Ferguson announced a $518 million settlement with several major opioid distributors aimed at resolving opioid-related claims. Following City Council approval on July 5, 2023, approximately $1.8M of these funds will be allocated to the City of Port Angeles over the next 18 years to address the opioid crisis, including substance use treatment, access to overdose-reversal drugs, and services to assist with those struggling with addition.

Goals and Scope

The City's goal when distributing these funds is to reduce the impact to public safety services, healthcare facilities, local businesses, and our City residents and visitors through effective and strategic measures, such as treatment, prevention, and education on opioid use and abuse.
