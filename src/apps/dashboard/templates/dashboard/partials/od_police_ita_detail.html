Why This Matters

Involuntary Treatment Act (ITA) holds represent the most severe intervention at an overdose scene. They indicate an immediate danger to self or others, often due to substance-induced psychosis or severe impairment.

Crisis Severity

A high rate of ITAs suggests a patient population in acute crisis, potentially requiring more intensive psychiatric and substance use stabilization services than standard outreach can provide.

System Capacity

Frequent ITAs can strain emergency departments and designated crisis responders. Monitoring these trends helps advocate for appropriate crisis stabilization facilities and diversion programs.

Strategic Action

Review cases with repeated ITAs for intensive case management. Collaborate with law enforcement and crisis responders to ensure ITAs are used appropriately and that patients are connected to long-term care.
