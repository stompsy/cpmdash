Strategic Insights

Why This Matters

Clinical Severity Indicator: CPR administration marks the most critical overdose presentations—cardiac or respiratory arrest has occurred. These cases represent the thin line between life and death, where every second counts and bystander intervention can make the difference.

Dual Intervention Strategy: The combination of CPR and naloxone addresses both the immediate circulatory failure and the underlying opioid toxicity. CPR maintains blood flow to vital organs while naloxone reverses respiratory depression—neither works optimally without the other in severe cases.

Prevention Imperative: High CPR rates signal that overdoses are reaching emergency services too late. This underscores the need for upstream interventions: wider naloxone distribution, bystander CPR training, and rapid response protocols.

Action Items: Train family members of at-risk individuals in CPR. Track time-to-intervention metrics. Partner with EMS to analyze response times in high-CPR zones.
