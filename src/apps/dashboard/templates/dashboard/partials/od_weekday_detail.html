How to Use Weekly Patterns

Understanding weekly patterns helps you match staffing and outreach to reality. If overdoses spike on weekends or specific evenings, that is a signal to shift CPM coverage, partner with shelters and first responders on those days, and make sure naloxone is stocked where people actually are.

Higher fatal rates on certain days may indicate delayed discovery, thinner EMS coverage, or fewer people around to call 911. Use those patterns to ask concrete questions: Do we need later hours? Different check-in routes? Standing weekend drop-ins? The goal is not to fill every hour evenlyit is to put your highest-skill staff where the data says risk quietly piles up.
