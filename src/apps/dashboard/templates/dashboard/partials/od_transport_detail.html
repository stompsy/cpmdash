Strategic Insights

Why This Matters

System Load Distribution: Transport destination patterns reveal which hospitals bear the overdose burden. This isn't just logistics—it's strategic intelligence for building ED partnerships, negotiating warm handoff protocols, and positioning peer recovery specialists where they'll see the most volume.

Geography and Access: Transport method distribution (EMS vs private vehicle vs air ambulance) maps the rural-urban divide and case severity gradients. Private vehicle transport may indicate either minor cases or areas where 911 carries stigma. Air ambulance use signals critical cases in geographically isolated areas.

The Non-Transport Opportunity: Every transport refusal is a chance for CP to prove its value. Traditional EMS has binary options: transport or don't. CP creates a third pathway—on-scene harm reduction, scheduled follow-up, connection to MAT, wellness checks—keeping people in a care relationship without the ER bottleneck.

Action Items: Partner with high-volume EDs for warm handoffs. Track transport-refusal cases for CP follow-up. Analyze fatal-transported cases to identify response-time gaps.
