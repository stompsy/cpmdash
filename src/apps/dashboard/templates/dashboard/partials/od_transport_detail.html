Why This Matters

Transport data reveals the immediate outcome of the overdose event. It tells us whether patients are accepting medical care, being arrested, or refusing transport against medical advice.

Hospital Utilization

High transport rates to EDs indicate severe overdoses or a lack of alternative stabilization options. "Treat and release" on scene (non-transport) can be safe with naloxone but requires robust follow-up.

Refusal of Care

Patients refusing transport (AMA) are at high risk for re-overdose as naloxone wears off. This group is a prime target for immediate community paramedicine follow-up.

Strategic Action

Develop "leave behind" naloxone kits and warm handoff protocols for patients who refuse transport. Ensure EDs have peer navigators to connect transported patients to treatment.
