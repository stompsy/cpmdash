Why Track Cost Savings?

Quantifying the financial impact of Community Paramedic interventions provides critical evidence for program sustainability, expansion, and community value.

  • Grant Justification. The nearly $2 million in documented savings across four years provides compelling evidence for continued funding and program expansion requests.
  • Resource Optimization. Understanding where savings occur (911 calls, transports, ED visits) helps optimize CPM deployment and identify high-impact intervention points.
  • Community Value Demonstration. Cost savings data helps communicate program benefits to stakeholders, city councils, and the public in tangible financial terms.
  • Strategic Decision Making. Trend analysis of savings across years (2021-2024) informs long-term planning and helps identify optimal patient populations for intervention.

Conservative Calculation Methodology

Our cost savings calculations use conservative estimates based on actual patient behavior changes following CPM intervention. In 2024, with 407 total patients served, these calculations demonstrate measurable financial impact while maintaining methodological rigor.

Preventing 911 Calls & Transports

In 2024, 42% of CPM patients had used 911 services. Of these, 71% (121 patients) showed reduced 911 usage after intervention. Conservative estimates project preventing 118 calls and 68 transports.

911 Response Without Transport

53 calls prevented × $1,900 per response

= $100,700 saved

911 Response With Transport

68 transports prevented × $3,800 per transport

= $258,400 saved

Based on 56% transport rate for 911 calls

Preventing Emergency Department Visits

An average of 51% of CPM patients visited the ED in 2024. Of these, 69% (144 patients) had reduced ED usage after intervention, preventing 144 visits through community-based care alternatives.

Emergency Department Visits Prevented

144 visits prevented × $1,146 per visit

= $165,024 saved

Cost includes exam and physician only
