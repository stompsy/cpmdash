Why Track Repeat Overdoses?

Repeat overdoses signal critical gaps in care and provide crucial insights into intervention effectiveness and patient vulnerability patterns.

  • Critical Intervention Gap. Only 3% of overdose survivors linked to services when CPMs didn't respond—versus 78% when they did.
  • Bridge to Recovery. CPMs act as a critical bridge for survivors during moments of readiness for change.
  • Predictive Analytics. Tracking recurrence helps predict which individuals may need intense case management.
  • System Indicators. Visualizing overdose intervals can indicate jail releases or post-discharge instability.

Pattern Recognition

Understanding repeat overdose patterns allows for targeted interventions, resource allocation, and identification of high-risk periods that may correlate with external factors like incarceration cycles or treatment disruptions.

Case Management Strategy

By analyzing repeat overdose timelines, Community Paramedics can develop personalized intervention strategies, optimize follow-up schedules, and coordinate with partner agencies to provide continuous support during vulnerable periods.

Key Insights from Repeat Overdose Analysis

High-Risk Patterns

Patients with shorter intervals between overdoses often represent higher acuity cases requiring immediate, intensive intervention and continuous monitoring.

System Gaps

Long gaps between overdoses may indicate periods of incarceration, hospitalization, or successful treatment—requiring different follow-up strategies upon return.

Intervention Timing

Understanding individual patient patterns enables CPMs to time interventions strategically, particularly during vulnerable transition periods.
