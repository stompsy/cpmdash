About the program

Who we are

The Community Paramedicine program launched in 2019 to meet residents where traditional systems left gaps. Today our team blends field medicine, behavioral health expertise, and care navigation to create a calmer path between a 9-1-1 call and long-term stability. We work shoulder to shoulder with trusted partners so neighbors receive home visits, post-overdose follow-up, chronic disease support, and harm reduction supplies without starting over at every door.

Every encounter is relationship-centered. We focus on medication alignment, safety assessments, wound care, vaccinations, and the warm handoffs that keep people connected to services they trust. Our work is as much about restoring confidence as it is about providing care.

“Wonderful people, providing exceptional service, to create a safer and healthier Port Angeles.” Fire Chief Derrell Sharp

How we care

Guiding residents from crisis to stability

Post-overdose stabilization

Operation Shielding Hope

Community Paramedics respond alongside EMS and law enforcement, helping patients stabilize, manage precipitated withdrawal, and enter medication assisted treatment with compassion. Warm handoffs to outreach partners begin before crews clear the scene.

Continuity at home

In-home clinical support

Residents receive home visits, telehealth touchpoints, and coaching that keep care on track. From medication reconciliation to wound care and chronic disease monitoring, CPMs bridge the gap until long-term providers can step in.

Care navigation

Bridging to community partners

A dedicated office tracks referrals, follow-up calls, and data sharing with case managers. We coordinate transportation, documentation, and handoffs so patients arrive where they are expected and feel welcomed when they get there.

Field rhythm

What the post-overdose journey feels like

When an overdose call goes out, a Community Paramedic unit launches with ALS equipment, buprenorphine, and the time to stay. Once life threats are managed, CPMs gain consent, listen, and prepare next steps while EMS units return to service.

  • 1 911 dispatch alerts EMS, law enforcement, and the PORT unit simultaneously to reduce delays in specialized support.
  • 2 On scene, CPMs manage precipitated withdrawal, provide harm reduction supplies, and invite preferred outreach partners to meet the patient where they are.
  • 3 Within 24 hours, staff confirm referrals, document touchpoints, and coordinate follow-up so the patient is not navigating recovery alone.

What stays with us

Zero repeat overdoses among the PORT referrals tracked in early 2025. Families tell us the difference is not a single medication – it is how long we stay and who we bring with us.
