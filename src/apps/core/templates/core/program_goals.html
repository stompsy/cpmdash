Pillars of the Program

⚡Post Overdose Response

Rapid outreach, MOUD navigation, prevention of precipitated withdrawal, and warm handoffs after overdose events.

✔High-Utilizer Support

Care plans, home visits, and coordination that reduce preventable 911/ED utilization.

❤Harm Reduction & Education

Naloxone distribution, fentanyl test strips, and field-based education for patients and families.

∞Coordinated Care Network

Close work with hospitals, clinics, behavioral health, housing, and peer organizations.

▣Data & Outcomes

Use dashboards and continuous improvement cycles to measure impact and guide strategy.

⊕Equity & Access

Center services on those with the highest barriers to care; reduce stigma and increase trust.

Roadmap

  2. 2021

    Pilot & initial outcomes

    Demonstrated reductions in ED/911 utilization and strong linkage-to-care.

  3. 2024

    Autonomous unit & POR

  4. 2027

    Scale & sustain

    Strengthen capacity, expand partnerships, and embed continuous improvement.
